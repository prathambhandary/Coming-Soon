A Life Remembered (Barely)

We gather today in solemn, barely-contained laughter to remember our dearly departed …, who left this world as suddenly as he left group chats — without warning, without explanation, and without replying to anyone's messages.

Those who knew him remember his signature aura — a complex bouquet of unwashed ambition, cold biryani, and an unshakeable belief that he was, somehow, the smartest person in any room he was currently eating in.

He is survived by his phone (8% battery, as always), a collection of downloaded movies he swore he'd watch, and a half-eaten packet of chips nobody has claimed. The fridge misses him. No one else does. (Kidding. Mostly.)

In his final hours, he reportedly said "bro wait I'm coming" — and then did not come. As was tradition.
