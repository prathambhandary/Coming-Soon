A Life Remembered (Barely)

We gather today in solemn, barely-contained laughter to remember our dearly departed …, who left this world as suddenly as he left group chats — without warning, without explanation, without replying.

Those who knew him remember his signature aura — a complex bouquet of unwashed ambition, cold biryani, and an unshakeable belief that he was the smartest person in any room he was eating in.

He is survived by his phone (8% battery, as always), movies he swore he'd watch, and a half-eaten packet of chips nobody has claimed. The fridge misses him. No one else does. (Kidding. Mostly.)

In his final hours, he reportedly said "bro wait I'm coming" — and then did not come. As was tradition.
